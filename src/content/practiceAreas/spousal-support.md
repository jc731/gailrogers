      <h2>Spousal Support Representation</h2>
      <p>Whether you are the payer or receiver, the issue of spousal support bears greatly on your financial well-being. I gather all the relevant facts to make a compelling case for fair and adequate support.</p>
      <h3>Seeking Spousal Support</h3>
      <p>If you are seeking spousal support, I will help you demonstrate your need for support and your spouse's ability to pay. I analyze factors such as:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Length of the marriage</li>
        <li>Each party's income and earning capacity</li>
        <li>Standard of living during the marriage</li>
        <li>Age and health of both parties</li>
        <li>Contributions to the marriage (financial and non-financial)</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Defending Against Spousal Support</h3>
      <p>If you are facing a spousal support claim, I will work to ensure that any support order is fair and based on accurate financial information. I will help you present evidence regarding:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Your actual income and ability to pay</li>
        <li>The other party's earning capacity</li>
        <li>Reasonable needs of both parties</li>
        <li>Other relevant factors under Illinois law</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Modification of Spousal Support</h3>
      <p>Spousal support orders can be modified if there is a substantial change in circumstances. I can help you seek a modification or defend against a modification request.</p>
faqs:
  - question: How is spousal support calculated in Illinois?
    answer: Illinois does not have a strict formula for spousal support. The court considers multiple factors including the length of the marriage, each party's income and earning capacity, the standard of living during the marriage, and the needs of each party.
  - question: How long does spousal support last?
    answer: The duration of spousal support depends on the circumstances of the case. It can be temporary, for a fixed period, or permanent. The court considers factors such as the length of the marriage and the parties' circumstances.
  - question: Can spousal support be modified?
    answer: Yes, spousal support can be modified if there is a substantial change in circumstances, such as a significant change in income, remarriage of the recipient, or retirement of the payer.
