      <h3>How do I file for divorce in Illinois?</h3>
      <p>To file for divorce in Illinois, you must meet residency requirements and file a petition for dissolution of marriage with the circuit court in the county where you or your spouse resides. The process involves:</p>
      <ol>
        <li>Meeting residency requirements (at least 90 days in Illinois)</li>
        <li>Filing a petition for dissolution of marriage</li>
        <li>Serving your spouse with the petition</li>
        <li>Completing financial disclosures</li>
        <li>Negotiating or litigating issues such as property division, support, and custody</li>
        <li>Obtaining a final judgment of dissolution</li>
      </ol>
      <p>Illinois is a no-fault divorce state, meaning you don't need to prove fault to get a divorce. The court can grant a divorce if the marriage has broken down irretrievably.</p>
      <p>I can guide you through each step of the process and help ensure your rights are protected.</p>
